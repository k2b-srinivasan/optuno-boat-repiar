Boat Restoration Miami

Complete mechanical, structural, and cosmetic restoration for boats of all ages

Complete Boat Restoration in Miami

Boat restoration is one of the most rewarding services we provide. Whether you've inherited a classic that hasn't run in years, bought a neglected project boat at a great price, or simply want to bring an aging vessel back to peak condition, our restoration team handles every aspect of the work from hull to helm. Miami's active resale market and the abundance of older fiberglass boats in South Florida keep our restoration shop busy year-round.

What Boat Restoration Includes

  • Structural assessment: Complete hull inspection, moisture readings, and evaluation of transom, stringers, and deck core integrity
  • Fiberglass and structural repair: Transom replacement, stringer repair, deck core replacement, and full laminate restoration
  • Engine and mechanical refit: Engine rebuild or replacement, fuel system overhaul, cooling system restoration, and drive service
  • Electrical refit: Full rewiring, new switches and panels, battery systems, and electronics installation
  • Cosmetic restoration: Gelcoat or paint, new non-skid, SeaDek flooring, teak restoration, and hardware polishing
  • Interior refit: New upholstery, canvas, carpet, headliner, and cabin surfaces

Classic Boat Restoration

South Florida has a rich history of classic fiberglass and wooden boats. We specialize in the restoration of classic center consoles, sportfishers, and runabouts from the 1960s through the 1990s — including brands like Boston Whaler, Mako, Aquasport, Bertram, and early Grady-White. Restoring a classic well requires understanding original construction methods and materials, sourcing period-correct or high-quality replacement parts, and knowing which modern upgrades improve the boat without compromising its character.

Project Scope & Budget Planning

The first step in any restoration is an honest assessment of what the boat needs and what it will cost. We provide a detailed written evaluation covering structural condition, mechanical needs, and cosmetic work required, along with a phased cost estimate that helps you plan. Some owners complete a full restoration in one project. Others do it in phases — structural and mechanical first, cosmetic second. We work with your timeline and budget to achieve the best outcome.

Our Boat Restoration Process

1

Full Survey & Scope

We conduct a complete survey of the vessel including moisture readings, mechanical inspection, and structural assessment. You receive a written report and phased estimate before any work begins.

2

Structural & Mechanical Work

Foundation-level work happens first: transom, stringers, and deck core if needed, followed by engine and mechanical systems. Nothing cosmetic goes on until the structure and machinery are sorted.

3

Systems & Electrical

Fuel system, electrical, plumbing, and mechanical systems are installed or overhauled. This is the stage where modern upgrades are integrated.

4

Cosmetic Finishing

Gelcoat or paint, SeaDek, upholstery, canvas, teak, and hardware complete the restoration. The final inspection verifies every system works before the boat goes back in the water.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I know if a boat is worth restoring?

The answer depends on the boat's intrinsic value (model, size, hull condition), the cost of restoration, and the finished value. We provide honest assessments — sometimes restoration makes great financial and sentimental sense, and sometimes buying a newer boat is the smarter move. We'll tell you which situation you're in.

Can you restore a boat with a rotten transom?

Yes. Transom replacement is one of the most common structural restoration jobs in South Florida. We remove the original fiberglass skin, replace the waterlogged wood core with marine-grade plywood or composite material, and re-glass over it. The finished transom is typically stronger than original.

How long does a full boat restoration take?

A complete restoration — structural, mechanical, and cosmetic — typically takes 2–6 months depending on the boat's size, condition, and the scope of work. We give you a timeline estimate after the initial assessment.

Do you work on wooden boats?

We handle fiberglass restorations primarily, but we work with wood elements within fiberglass boats (transoms, stringers, teak decks, cabin joinery). For dedicated wooden hull restorations, we can provide a referral to a specialist.
